Reading List

Topic 6: Poverty and Social Policy

Poverty and social exclusion matter because they affect fundamentally both individuals’ life chances and the kind of society that everyone lives in. This topic explores these concepts, including issues of definition and measurement, approaches to dealing with poverty, and key topical issues focusing on recent UK developments such as universal credit and benefit caps.

Sample Questions

  1. Does the capabilities approach resolve the choice between focusing on absolute poverty or relative poverty?
  2. Are targeted benefits an effective means to reduce poverty?
  3. Explain how the idea that child poverty “is about more than income; it is about a lack of opportunity, aspiration and stability” (DWP, 2011) has informed the evolution of UK policy towards poor children.
