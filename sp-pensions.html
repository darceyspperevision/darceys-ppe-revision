Reading List

Topic 9: Old Age Pensions

Old-age pensions are typically the largest item of social expenditure in OECD countries. Demographic change has unsurprisingly been an important driver of pension reform, but it is far from the only factor. This session explores various strategies that countries have used in protecting elderly incomes, some of their main drivers and outcomes in terms of poverty, inequality, and sustainability.

Sample Questions

  1. Are recent pension reforms in the UK likely to reduce or increase inequalities amongst pensioners?
  2. Do recent changes in pensions policy in the UK threaten intergenerational solidarity?
  3. Does population ageing threaten the sustainability of pension systems?
