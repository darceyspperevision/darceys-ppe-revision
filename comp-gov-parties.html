Reading List

Political Parties and Party Systems

Organized political parties are found in all contemporary democracies. Typically parties mobilise mass electorates, compete for public office, and some then engage in government. Questions about parties involve: how parties are formed and link with particular groups in society; how parties organise for competing for power; and the logic of party competition and co-operation and differences in party system behaviour across democratic regimes.

Essay Questions

  1. Is there an increasing tendency for parties to converge on the political centre-ground?
  2. How can change in party systems best be explained?
