Immigration and Social Policy

Reading List

Topic 4: Immigration and Social Policy

Immigration is a contested issue in contemporary politics and a key challenge for the future of the European welfare states. This topic introduces dominant discourses and debates surrounding migration and social policy, and critically examines different approaches to the provision of migrants’ social rights. Both perceived and real economic, social and political impacts of migration will be explored.

Sample Questions

  1. Is it possible to combine an inclusive welfare state with relatively high levels of immigration?
  2. Does immigration erode public support for the welfare state?
  3. How adequate are current approaches and concepts used to study social policy for the analysis of immigrants’ social rights?
