Families, Care and Social Policies

Reading List

Topic 10: Families, Care and Social Policies

There has been much debate about family change, gender inequality and rising care needs in recent years, and issues of how to support families and carers have risen up the policy agenda, in part due to demographic, social and economic changes. This topic explores policy approaches towards ‘children’, ‘ageing’ and ‘care’, with a particular focus on debates and policy initiatives relating to children’s outcomes, gender inequalities and unmet care needs.

Sample Questions

  1. To which extent have childcare policies moved towards a child-centred approach in the UK?
  2. Assess the impact of recent reforms of childcare policy on women in the UK.
  3. Critically assess the role of choice in recent reforms of adult social care in the UK.
