Cadham & Prosser (2023)

Cadham, C. J. and Prosser, L. A. (2023) “Eliciting Trade-Offs Between Equity and Efficiency: A Methodological Scoping Review”, Value in Health, 26 (6): 948–958.

Overview

A systematic methodological scoping review of 77 survey-based studies from 29 countries, investigating how societies quantify the trade-off between equity (fairness/prioritising the worst-off) and efficiency (maximising overall health outcomes cost-effectively) in healthcare resource allocation. Divided into two approaches: Social Welfare Function approaches (27 studies: ask respondents to trade off benefits across groups to calculate an “inequality aversion parameter”; 20/27 found clear inequality aversion) and Preference Ranking and Distributional Weighting (49 studies: more mixed results). Conclusion: societies generally demonstrate willingness to sacrifice some efficiency for equity, but massive methodological heterogeneity limits policy applicability.

Key Arguments & Evidence

Society generally values equity and is willing to sacrifice efficiency for it.
Across 77 studies: 39 showed clear evidence of inequality aversion, 33 mixed, only 4 found no inequality aversion. In social welfare function studies specifically, 20/27 showed distinct aversion.
Methodological heterogeneity limits direct comparability and policy usefulness.
Survey methods ranged from discrete choice experiments (23 studies) to “leaky bucket” scenarios. Sample sizes ranged from 10 to 4,118; many used non-representative samples (students, health policy experts).
Preferences for equity are highly heterogenous and depend on sociodemographic context.
28/39 studies evaluating demographic differences found statistically significant variation. Conservative, higher-income, and male respondents were often less averse to inequality.

Key Concepts & Definitions

Utilitarian Approach
Resource allocation framework favouring interventions with maximum overall health benefits at lowest cost, regardless of distribution.
Cost-Effectiveness Analysis (CEA)
Economic evaluation comparing an intervention’s costs to health benefits produced.
Distributional / Extended CEA
Modern CEA measuring distribution of outcomes across different socioeconomic groups, applying equity weights.
Social Welfare Function
Mathematical approach quantifying how much a society cares about reducing distributional inequality.
Inequality Aversion
Quantifiable metric specifying how much an individual or society cares about reducing differences in outcome distribution.
Veil of Ignorance
Survey technique asking respondents to make allocation choices without knowing their own future position in the distribution.

On the Essay Question

✦ Have recent changes in the welfare mix in the UK resulted more from concerns about efficiency than from concerns about equality? ✦

  • Source cannot directly address UK welfare mix changes: This is a methodological review of survey data, not a historical/political analysis of UK policy shifts.
  • But UK public preferences lean toward equity: The UK was the most studied location (24/77 studies). Of these, 10 showed clear inequality aversion (equity preference), 12 mixed, only 2 showed efficiency-only preferences.
  • Conclusion from this source: While it cannot tell us why the government has altered the welfare mix, it definitively shows that when UK citizens and policymakers are polled, there is a distinct prevailing desire to ensure equality — suggesting efficiency-driven changes may be running against public preferences.
