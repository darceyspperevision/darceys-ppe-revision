Interest Groups

Interest groups are crucially important links between the state and society. Some scholars argue they are essential to political representation; others that they risk subverting it. Academic debates raise normative questions about resource equality, the relationship between state and society, the location of political power, and the transparency and quality of participation in policy-making. The main areas of study are: the ‘collective action problem’ and what it implies about interest group formation; and the interaction between interest groups and the state through frameworks of corporatism and pluralism.

Essay Questions

  1. Why has it proved difficult to formulate general comparative conclusions about interest groups?
  2. To what extent does the literature on the ‘logic of collective action’ explain the distribution of power within and between interests?
