Reading List

Topic 5: Health Policy

Historical, economic, political and ideological influences combine to determine the design and operation of a health system. We examine historic and current challenges facing the UK’s National Health Service, and how far UK healthcare reforms over recent decades were driven by economic/fiscal and/or political/ideological imperatives. We also ask about the fundamental causes of health inequalities and about what policies are effective in reducing them.

Sample Questions

  1. To what extent are the labels ‘national’, ‘social’ and ‘private’ still useful in analysing health systems in advanced economies — and to what extent does the current UK NHS retain the essential characteristics of the service designed in 1948?
  2. Health inequalities are not best addressed by health policies. Discuss.
  3. Given the principle of equal access to health care, how do we make sense of continued health inequalities in NHS systems?
