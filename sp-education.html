Reading List

Topic 8: Education Policy

Education reforms have been a key area of policy change in the UK, as part of raising standards, achieving economic competitiveness and tackling social inequalities in educational attainment. This session examines the historical origins and contemporary policy development of education, focusing in particular on contemporary policy approaches emphasising parental choice, the national curriculum and new forms of governance, accountability and policy evaluation.

Sample Questions

  1. Is individual choice a meaningful concept in schools’ policy in the UK today?
  2. What has been the impact of education policies on the achievement gap?
  3. Why are educational inequalities so difficult to eradicate?
